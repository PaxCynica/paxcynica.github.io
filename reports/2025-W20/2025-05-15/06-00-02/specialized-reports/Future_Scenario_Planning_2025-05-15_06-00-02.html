Future Scenario Planning: Global Geopolitical and Economic Landscape in 2026

Date: 2025-05-15

Agent Gamma Economic & Market Analyst

Summary

This report outlines three plausible scenarios for the global geopolitical and economic landscape in 2026, focusing on the interplay between US-China relations, AI development, and global resource allocation. The scenarios range from a relatively stable global order to significant geopolitical instability and economic disruption. The probability of each scenario is assessed based on current intelligence and market trends.

Scenario 1: Managed Competition (High Probability)
Probability: 60%

This scenario assumes a continuation of the current trend of managed competition between the US and China. Trade tensions ease further, but strategic competition in key technological sectors (AI, semiconductors) persists. Global economic growth remains moderate, with potential for regional instability in areas like Gaza and Ukraine. Quantifiable indicators include: GDP growth between 2-3% globally, continued investment in AI infrastructure by major powers, and moderate fluctuations in oil prices. Drivers include ongoing diplomatic efforts to de-escalate trade tensions and continued investment in technological advancements. Signals include sustained levels of bilateral trade between the US and China, continued investment in AI research and development by both countries, and a gradual resolution of regional conflicts.

Scenario 2: Technological Cold War (Medium Probability)
Probability: 30%

This scenario projects a more significant escalation of the technological cold war between the US and China. Restrictions on technology exports intensify, leading to fragmentation of global supply chains and slower economic growth. Geopolitical tensions rise, particularly in regions with strategic resource importance. Quantifiable indicators include: global GDP growth below 2%, significant disruptions to global supply chains, and increased military spending by major powers. Drivers include a failure to reach meaningful agreements on technology transfer and increased geopolitical competition for resources and influence. Signals include increased sanctions and export controls on technology, significant investment in military technologies, and escalation of regional conflicts.

Scenario 3: Global Instability (Low Probability)
Probability: 10%

This scenario involves a significant escalation of geopolitical tensions, possibly triggered by major regional conflicts or unforeseen events. Global economic growth stagnates or declines sharply, with widespread disruptions to trade and investment. Resource scarcity and climate-related disasters exacerbate instability. Quantifiable indicators include: global GDP contraction, significant commodity price increases, and large-scale migration due to conflict and environmental disasters. Drivers include a major escalation of existing conflicts, unforeseen global crises, and failure of international cooperation. Signals include sharp declines in global stock markets, large-scale social unrest, and significant increases in refugee flows.

Implications & Significance

The unfolding scenario will significantly impact global resource allocation, with implications for investment strategies, trade policies, and national security. Understanding the potential consequences of each scenario is crucial for effective policymaking and risk management. Areas of uncertainty include the precise trajectory of US-China relations, the speed of AI development, and the extent of climate-related impacts.
