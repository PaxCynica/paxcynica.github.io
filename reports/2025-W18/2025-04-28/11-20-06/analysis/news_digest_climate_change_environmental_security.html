Climate Change & Environmental Security Briefing

Prepared on 2025-04-28

Key Themes & Analysis

1. Impacts of Climate Change on Food Security and Public Health

Keywords: Climate change, global warming, foodborne illnesses, food spoilage, World Health Organization
Implications: Rising temperatures and extreme weather events are increasing food spoilage and the risk of foodborne illnesses, impacting public health and exacerbating existing inequalities in food access. This poses significant challenges for global food security and requires urgent action to mitigate climate change and improve food safety practices.
  • Climate change is spoiling food faster, making hundreds of millions of people sick around the world

2. Political Polarization and Climate Action

Keywords: Climate change, climate policy, Trump administration, John Kerry, Paris Agreement, political polarization
Implications: Deep political divisions continue to hinder effective climate action. The dismantling of climate initiatives by the Trump administration and contrasting viewpoints on the severity and causes of climate change create significant obstacles to international cooperation and the implementation of necessary policies.
  • Trump administration scraps Kerry’s climate office, citing ideological overreach
  • Climate Myths, Continued
  • KEVIN MOONEY: Trump Makes America Independent From The Climate Grift

3. Extreme Weather Events and their Consequences

Keywords: Extreme weather, wildfires, drought, flooding, heat waves, Himalayan snow, UK wildfires
Implications: The increasing frequency and intensity of extreme weather events are causing widespread damage, displacement, and loss of life. These events significantly impact infrastructure, economies, and human well-being, highlighting the urgent need for climate adaptation measures and disaster preparedness.
  • Snow hits 23-year low in the Himalayas — now it is threatening 2 billion people
  • Area burned by UK wildfires in 2025 already at annual record
  • U.S. National Weather Service Advises Disney Vacation Cancelation, Record-Breaking Heat and Wildfires

4. Technological Advancements in Climate Mitigation and Adaptation

Keywords: Green energy transition, solar power, clean energy, water purification technology, AI for climate, biomimicry
Implications: Technological innovations offer promising solutions for mitigating climate change and adapting to its effects. Advancements in renewable energy, water purification, and AI-driven climate modeling hold the potential to significantly reduce greenhouse gas emissions and improve resilience to climate impacts. However, widespread adoption and equitable access remain significant challenges.
  • Solar Power Surges Around The World
  • Scientists spark major breakthrough with 'blanket' that makes rivers drinkable
  • 4 Ways Technology Is Powering Sustainability At 2025 London Marathon

5. Continued Reliance on Fossil Fuels and Subsidies

Keywords: Fossil fuels, subsidies, G20, clean energy, carbon emissions
Implications: Despite growing awareness of climate change, continued government subsidies for fossil fuels hinder the transition to cleaner energy sources. This underscores the need for policy reforms that prioritize sustainable energy investments and phase out fossil fuel support. The role of the G20 in addressing this issue is critical.
  • Fossil fuels are still subsidised: G20 could push for the funds to be shifted to cleaner energy
  • The six things you should know about gas and if it can make power cheaper
