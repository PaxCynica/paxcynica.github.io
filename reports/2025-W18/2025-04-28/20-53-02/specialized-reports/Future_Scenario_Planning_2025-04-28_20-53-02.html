Future Scenario Planning: Global AI Competition and Economic Instability

Date: 2025-04-28

Agent Gamma Economic & Market Analyst

Summary

This report outlines three plausible scenarios for 2026-2027, focusing on the interplay between global AI competition (particularly between the US and China) and broader economic instability. The scenarios consider varying levels of success in AI development, the impact of geopolitical tensions, and the resulting resource allocation shifts. While significant uncertainty remains, a data-driven approach highlights potential outcomes and key indicators to watch.

Scenario 1: US AI Dominance and Global Economic Recovery Probability: Medium

Drivers: Significant breakthroughs in US AI development, leading to a technological advantage in key sectors. Increased international cooperation on AI safety and ethical considerations. Easing of geopolitical tensions, leading to improved global trade.

Quantifiable Indicators: US GDP growth above 3%, global trade volume increase of 5%, Nvidia market capitalization exceeding $3 trillion, reduced global military spending.

Signals: Increased foreign investment in US tech firms, successful international AI safety agreements, reduction in trade disputes, positive global consumer confidence.

Assumptions: Continued US government investment in AI research and development, effective international cooperation on AI governance, a decline in global conflict.

Consequences: Increased US global economic and technological influence, potential for job displacement in other countries, enhanced global economic stability.

Scenario 2: Stalemate and Persistent Economic Uncertainty Probability: High

Drivers: Continued intense competition between the US and China in AI development, no clear technological leader emerges. Persistent geopolitical tensions, leading to trade disputes and supply chain disruptions.

Quantifiable Indicators: Global GDP growth below 2%, high inflation rates (above 4%), volatile stock markets, increased military spending in multiple regions.

Signals: Continued trade disputes between major powers, cyberattacks targeting critical infrastructure, increased volatility in global financial markets, escalation of regional conflicts.

Assumptions: No significant breakthroughs in AI development by either side, continued geopolitical rivalry, failure to reach international agreements on AI governance.

Consequences: Prolonged economic uncertainty, increased inequality, heightened global security risks, potential for regional conflicts to escalate.

Scenario 3: Chinese AI Leadership and Global Economic Fragmentation Probability: Low

Drivers: Significant breakthroughs in Chinese AI development, leading to a technological advantage in key sectors. Increased geopolitical tensions, leading to technological decoupling and economic fragmentation.

Quantifiable Indicators: Significant decline in US GDP growth, increased global trade imbalances, Huawei market capitalization exceeding $4 trillion, sharp increase in global military spending.

Signals: Increased Chinese global influence, significant shifts in global supply chains, sanctions and trade wars between major powers, formation of competing economic blocs.

Assumptions: Successful Chinese AI development, escalation of geopolitical tensions, a failure to mitigate the negative consequences of AI development.

Consequences: Shift in global economic power, increased global instability, potential for increased regional conflicts, significant disruption to global trade and supply chains.

Implications & Significance

The unfolding scenarios highlight the critical importance of proactive strategies to manage the risks and harness the opportunities presented by the rapid advancement of AI. International cooperation, robust regulatory frameworks, and investments in education and workforce development are crucial to mitigate potential negative consequences and ensure a more equitable and stable future.
