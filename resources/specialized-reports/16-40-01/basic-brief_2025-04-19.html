The US and China continue to experience tension across military and economic domains. The US is pushing back against China's influence in the East, while trade disputes and tariffs are causing economic uncertainty. This rivalry has the potential to destabilize international relations and disrupt global trade, with China potentially trying to cripple the US economy by selling US debt. 3. Geopolitical Implications of Space-Based Systems

The development of space-based missile defense systems, particularly by companies like SpaceX, raises questions about the weaponization of space and potential violations of international treaties. Subscription-based access models for defense technologies are emerging, potentially impacting government procurement and control. This development is occurring amidst rising military rivalry in the Middle East. The frequency and sophistication of cyberattacks are increasing, highlighting the vulnerability of organizations and individuals. Recent incidents include data breaches at Legends International and a ransomware breach at a major US supermarket chain. Vulnerabilities in ASUS routers and Apple products are also being exploited. 5. Economic Uncertainty: Tariffs and Stagflation Concerns

The potential for stagflation, driven by tariffs and trade tensions, is raising concerns about rising unemployment, slower growth, and increased market volatility. President Trump's trade policies and attacks on the Federal Reserve are creating economic uncertainty and potentially undermining investor confidence. Companies are pulling products from the US due to rising tariffs. The ongoing conflict and blockade in Gaza are creating a severe humanitarian crisis, particularly affecting children, who are surviving on less than a meal a day. Deadly Israeli strikes continue despite attempted ceasefires, exacerbating the crisis. There are also concerns about erosion of humanitarian law and international norms.
