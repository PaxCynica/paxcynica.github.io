China is actively expanding its influence in Southeast Asia through infrastructure projects, diplomatic visits, and trade agreements. This increasing presence raises concerns about regional power dynamics and potential debt traps for smaller nations. Countries in the region are carefully balancing their relationships with China and other major powers.

Implications: Shifting regional power balance. Increased competition between China and other major powers (US, Japan, India) for influence. Potential for disputes over territorial claims and resources.

Nigeria continues to grapple with political instability and violence, including deadly clashes between herders and farmers. These conflicts are exacerbating existing social and economic challenges. The government is struggling to maintain security and address the root causes of the violence.

Taiwan is facing increasing security concerns due to rising tensions with China, coupled with uncertainties surrounding international trade policies. These actions reflect a growing awareness of the multifaceted threats facing the island.
