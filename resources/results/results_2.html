Politics & War News Digest: April 17, 2025

Major Themes & Key Trends

1. Israel-Palestine Conflict: Deepening Gaza Crisis and International Pressure

The humanitarian situation in Gaza continues to deteriorate sharply, with Israel maintaining its blockade and reportedly using aid as a "pressure lever." Dozens, including children, were killed in recent strikes on tent camps, even in designated "safe zones." International calls for a ceasefire persist (e.g., French President Macron), while the human cost is highlighted by the World Press Photo award going to an image of an injured Gazan boy. Israel's defense minister stated troops will remain indefinitely in Gaza, Lebanon, and Syria, signaling a hardening stance.

Keywords: Gaza, Israel, Palestine, humanitarian aid, blockade, ceasefire, casualties, World Press Photo, Macron, indefinite occupation

  • Hamas accuses Israel of weaponising aid as Gaza’s hunger crisis worsens (Al Jazeera)
  • Israel kills children in attacks on tents including Gaza’s “safe zone” (Al Jazeera)
  • Israeli Army to remain indefinitely in Gaza – defense minister (RT)
  • French President Macron demands Immediate Gaza Ceasefire, Resumption of Civilian Aid, Palestinian State (Juan Cole)
  • Palestinian Photojournalist Wins World Press Photo of the Year 2025 (PetaPixel)

Implications: The worsening humanitarian crisis and lack of a ceasefire increase the risk of broader regional instability and further international condemnation of Israel's actions. The indefinite occupation plans complicate any future peace prospects and raise questions about long-term governance in Gaza.

2. US Trade Policy Impacts: Tariffs, Business Adjustments, and ASEAN Response

The impact of US tariffs continues to ripple through global commerce. Companies like Shein and Temu are warning US customers of imminent price hikes due to tariffs. This trade pressure is influencing international investment flows, with wealthy Chinese buyers reportedly shifting focus from the US to Thai property. ASEAN nations, including Thailand, are considering a united front in negotiations with the US over these tariffs.

Keywords: tariffs, trade war, US trade policy, Shein, Temu, ASEAN, Thailand, China, price hikes, economic impact

  • Shein and Temu say you've got 8 days of low-cost shopping left before the Trump tariff price hikes kick in (Business Insider)
  • Thai PM backs Asean united front on tariffs (Bangkok Post)
  • Wealthy Chinese still keen on Thai property (Bangkok Post)
  • The complicated calculation of ending de minimis (Fashion Dive)

Implications: US tariffs are forcing businesses to adjust pricing and supply chains, potentially fueling inflation for consumers. They are also prompting regional blocs like ASEAN to coordinate responses, potentially altering global trade dynamics and investment patterns.

3. China's Regional Influence and Ongoing Tech Competition

China continues to exert influence in Southeast Asia, with President Xi Jinping visiting Cambodia seeking financial support amid the US tariff war. Beijing's stance on Taiwan remains firm, drawing protests from Taipei over a joint China-Vietnam statement. The US-China tech rivalry persists, evident in competition over cloud data storage dominance and Chinese developers eyeing the US market for low-altitude aircraft despite tariffs. Taiwan's TSMC remains a key player, with high profit expectations.

Keywords: China, Xi Jinping, Cambodia, Vietnam, Taiwan, ASEAN, tariffs, technology, cloud computing, semiconductors, TSMC, low-altitude aircraft

  • Cambodia seeks China’s financial support as Xi visits amid US tariff war (Al Jazeera)
  • Analyzing the PLA’s Early April Exercises in the Taiwan Strait (The Diplomat)
  • Where Chinese Or American Tech Is Used In Cloud Data Storage (Forbes)
  • Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSM): $10.74B Q1 Profit Expected Amid U.S. Tariff Threats (Yahoo Finance)
  • Chinese developers of low-altitude aircraft set sights on US, even with high tariffs (SCMP)

Implications: China's economic and diplomatic outreach in Southeast Asia aims to counter US pressure and secure regional partnerships. The ongoing tech competition and tensions over Taiwan remain significant flashpoints with global security and economic consequences.

4. Southeast Asian Diplomacy and Security Developments

Diplomatic activity is notable in Southeast Asia. Malaysia's Prime Minister is meeting Myanmar's junta chief in Bangkok to discuss ceasefire extensions, despite criticism. Thailand and Malaysia are progressing on a cross-border bridge project. Security cooperation continues with the large-scale Balikatan military exercises between the Philippines and the US commencing.

Keywords: Malaysia, Thailand, Myanmar, Philippines, ASEAN, diplomacy, ceasefire, Balikatan exercise, infrastructure

  • Malaysia PM set to meet Myanmar junta chief amid opposition to talks (Bangkok Post)
  • Malaysia, Thailand jointly funded cross-border bridge expected to be completed in 2028 (The Star Online)
  • Philippine, U.S. Troops Kick off Exercise Balikatan 2025 (GlobalSecurity.org)

Implications: Regional diplomacy aims to manage complex issues like the Myanmar crisis, though consensus remains challenging. Infrastructure projects signal ongoing economic integration, while joint military exercises underscore evolving security alignments in response to regional tensions.

5. African Business Landscape and Geopolitical Interests

The business environment in Africa sees shifts, with PwC exiting operations in nine Sub-Saharan countries to focus on other growth markets. Conversely, Russia is increasing its presence, with dozens of tech firms participating in the GITEX Africa exhibition in Morocco. Geopolitical interests are also evident, with the US potentially eyeing mineral resources in the Congo amid conflict and considering embassy closures elsewhere on the continent. Morocco is also potentially purchasing Stinger missiles from the US.

Keywords: Africa, PwC, Russia, Morocco, GITEX Africa, Congo, minerals, US diplomacy, Stinger missiles

  • PwC exits nine African countries, cites focus on growth markets (The Punch)
  • Dozens of Russian tech firms took part in GITEX Africa (RT)
  • Trump Eyes Congo's "Incredible Mineral Riches" as Armed Conflict Devastates Region (Democracy Now!)
  • US approves potential sale of Stinger Block I Missiles to Morocco worth $825 million (GlobalSecurity.org)

Implications: Africa remains a continent of strategic interest for global powers and businesses, leading to both investment and divestment based on strategic reviews and resource interests. External powers' engagement can influence regional stability and economic development trajectories.
